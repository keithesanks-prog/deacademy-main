Compute + Storage

Every computer (and every Cloud) does essentially TWO things.

It's like an inverse operation. They need each other.

Rob: To see how Compute and Storage work together, let's look at a simple math problem: 2 + 2.

Your brain thinks about the problem. That's Compute.
Then you write "4" on a piece of paper. That's Storage.

If you didn't have Storage (the paper), you'd forget the answer instantly!
If you didn't have Compute (your brain), the paper would just be blank!

They are the Yin and Yang of the digital world.

Part 1: AWS (The Giant Toolbox)

AWS is like a giant hardware store where you rent these two things separately.

The Brain (Compute)

These are the machines that DO the work.

  • EC2: A raw computer. You install Windows, you fix it when it breaks.
  • Lambda: A hired genius. It comes in, solves one math problem, and leaves.

The Paper (Storage)

These are the places that KEEP the work.

  • S3: infinite sticky notes. Write anything, never run out of space.
  • DynamoDB: A super-fast filing cabinet.

Rob: Whoa Dude! That's a lot of tools!
And that's why people get confused. They see 200 tools and panic.
But really, they are almost ALL just different versions of Brains or Paper.

Part 2: The Snowflake Twist ‚ùÑÔ∏è

Now, let's look at something called Snowflake. Is it Compute? Is it Storage?

It's BOTH, but they are divorced!

The Magic Trick üèõÔ∏è ‚ö° üè¢
Unified Data, Separate Computing

Imagine a Library (Storage/S3). It has one copy of every book.

Now imagine you can magically spawn 50 Study Rooms (Compute/Warehouses) next door.

The "Legacy" way: Every Study Room needed its own copy of the books. (Slow! Expensive!)

The Snowflake way: All rooms read from the SAME Library at the SAME time.

Rob: This is the "Aha!" moment.

In the old days, if the Marketing Team wanted to run a huge report, they would hog all the computers, and the Finance Team couldn't do any work.

In Snowflake, Marketing gets Warehouse A. Finance gets Warehouse B.
They are totally separate brains, but they look at the exact same memory.
Everyone is happy!

Summary

Alright... so now you know the difference between Compute and Storage.

  • The Cloud is just renting Brains and Paper.
  • AWS gives you the raw blocks to build your own castle.
  • Snowflake gives you a magical castle where the library is infinite and the study rooms can multiply.
