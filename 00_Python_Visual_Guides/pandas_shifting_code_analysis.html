Breaking down your time-series script line-by-line

df['sales_lagged_1'] = df['Sales'].shift(periods=1)
1. Lagging (Shift Down)

Goal: "What were sales yesterday?"

It takes the entire column and moves it DOWN by 1 step.

  • Top row becomes NaN (No yesterday).
  • Useful for: Calculating difference from previous day.
Index Sales (Original) sales_lagged_1

df['Sales_Leading_1'] = df['Sales'].shift(periods=-1)
2. Leading (Shift Up)

Goal: "What will sales be tomorrow?"

It takes the entire column and moves it UP by 1 step.

  • Bottom row becomes NaN (No tomorrow).
  • Useful for: Predicting next day's target.
Index Sales (Original) Sales_Leading_1
Day 1 100 ‚ÜñÔ∏è 120
Day 2 120 ‚ÜñÔ∏è 150
Day 3 150 ‚ÜñÔ∏è NaN
df['Percent'] = (df['Sales'].pct_change() * 100).round(2)
3. Percent Change

Goal: "How much did we grow?"

Combines shift(1) with math used automatically:

(New - Old) / Old

.round(2) keeps it clean (e.g., 20.0%).

Sales (New) Sales (Old/Shift 1) Calculation Result
100 NaN (100-NaN)/NaN NaN
120 100 (120-100)/100 20.00%
150 120 (150-120)/120 25.00%
