Lab 00: Stocking the Pantry (ECR)

Rob: "You can't cook without ingredients!
Before we build a Task Definition, we need to upload your custom image to the Private Pantry (ECR). Let's buy a shelf."
Step 1

Buy the Shelf (Create Repository)

ü•´ Analogy: Renting a specific shelf for your 'Soup'.

We need a named place to store your specific application images.

1. Go to Amazon ECR -> Repositories.
2. Click Create repository.
3. Visibility: Private.
4. Name: web-server-custom.
5. Click Create repository.
Step 2

Stock the Shelf (Push Image)

üèãÔ∏è Analogy: Putting the cans on the shelf.

This is the part everyone forgets. AWS gives you the cheat codes!

1. Select your new web-server-custom repo.
2. Click the View push commands button (Top Right).
3. Run those 4 commands in your local terminal (where your Dockerfile is).
The 4 commands usually do:
  1. Login: Authenticates your Docker client to AWS.
  2. Build: Cooks the image locally.
  3. Tag: Puts the AWS Sticker on it.
  4. Push: Uploads it to the cloud.
Step 3

Get the Label (The URI)

üè∑Ô∏è Analogy: The SKU Number.

Now that it's uploaded, you need the Magic Address to tell ECS where to find it.

1. Inside the Repository, look for the Image URI column.
2. Click the Copy icon next to latest.
Example: 123456789.dkr.ecr.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/web-server-custom:latest
Next Step: "Take this URI and paste it into the Image field of your Task Definition!"
