Deep Dive: Lateral Flatten

Rob: "People get confused by LATERAL. It just means 'Sideways'.
Instead of joining to another table, the query looks sideways at the current row's backpack (Array) and explodes it."

The Player

One Row (Order)

Holds a single hand involves of multiple cards: ['item1', 'item2'].

The Dealer

Lateral Flatten

Takes that one hand and deals each card onto the table as a new row.

The Visual: Explosion

BEFORE (The Hand): +----+---------------------+ | ID | ITEMS | +----+---------------------+ | 1 | ['item1', 'item2'] | <-- One Player, Two Cards +----+---------------------+ ‚¨áÔ∏è LATERAL FLATTEN ‚¨áÔ∏è AFTER (The Spread): +----+----------------+ | ID | FLATTENED_ITEM | +----+----------------+ | 1 | 'item1' | <-- Card 1 (ID Cloned) | 1 | 'item2' | <-- Card 2 (ID Cloned) +----+----------------+

The Code

Here is the exact magic used to do this.

SELECT o.ID, flattened_item.value -- The 'value' is the card itself FROM orders o, LATERAL FLATTEN(input => o.Items) AS flattened_item;
  • input: Where the Dealer looks for the hand (o.Items).
  • value: The individual card being placed on the table.
  • Lateral: Connects orders o to flatten(...) row-by-row.
