Who is Who? (The Corporate Analogy)

Snowflake roles act like job titles in a company. Just because you are the CEO doesn't mean you should fix the printer.

üëë ACCOUNTADMIN The CEO / Owner

Power: Infinite. Can do anything. Can see billing. Can delete the account.

Rule: üõë NEVER use this for daily work. Only use it to check bills or reset passwords if everyone is locked out.

üõ°Ô∏è SECURITYADMIN Head of HR & Security

Job: "Oh, a new employee joined? I will create their user."

üèóÔ∏è SYSADMIN Head of Engineering / Infrastructure

Power: Creates Warehouses (Servers), Databases, and Tables.

Job: "We need a new database for Finance." -> Call SysAdmin.

üë§ USERADMIN HR Assistant

‚úÖ The Golden Rule of Snowflake

If you look at the diagram arrows, they point UP.

This means SYSADMIN should carry the burden of creating objects (Databases/Tables).

Ultimately, you will create a custom role (e.g., DATA_ENGINEER) that reports to SYSADMIN.


Don't be the CEO (AccountAdmin) when you are just trying to build a table!
